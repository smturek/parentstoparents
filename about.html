About Us

Who We Are

We are a group of parents who have had a child or adolescent suffer through mental illness(es) and desire to help other parents who have a child with mental illness(es).

Why We Do What We Do

We feel driven to help other parents gain understanding of the mental illnesses’ impact on the brain and personality and to help them find evidence-based support as quickly as possible. At the time of our children’s struggles, we made mistakes. Many mistakes. We had no map of where to go; no idea which theories and therapies would work; and we were unaware of the research which supported specific theoretical perspectives over others. We desire to provide a "map" to help other parents.

Our Mission

Our goal is to provide clear, practical, and evidence‐based information and suggestions to parents on how to respond to mental illnesses, as well as criteria to consider when selecting a psychotherapist for the family and/or child for each mental illness. We hope to both empower parents with the knowledge and skills to respond to their adolescents mental illness(es) in compassionate yet effective ways; and to provide a synopsis of current research regarding each mental illness to help parents make informed, evidence‐based decisions regarding their treatment options.
